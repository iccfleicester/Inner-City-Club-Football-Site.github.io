About Us

Inner City Club Football Leicester (ICCF) was established in 2003 to create football opportunities for young people living in Highfields and the neighbouring areas. Seeing that there was no structured football available for youngsters, they were determined to set up an organisation which is solely based in the heart of the city.

Since 2003, the club has developed and now runs training sessions for age groups ranging between 5 to 16 year olds.

ICCF has an open policy. This club was set up for the children that have no club to play for and for players that are looking to improve their social and football skills.

Most of the work done by the members of ICCF is voluntarily. All coaches are willing to pass on their expertise to help improve the community. They are all passionate about their football and have realised there was no structured football for them when they were young, so they wanted to make a change for the youth of today.

ICCF aims to unify diversity across Leicester through football. ICCF is committed in its endeavour to provide quality coaching and education to build up 5-16-year-olds, with integrity and the ability to play football at a competitive level. Encouraging a modern, child-friendly approach to youth football, ICCF dedicates a challenging and learning mentality for children and young teens. Working together with a proactive attitude, Coaches at ICCF foster a learning environment for children that put their development needs at the centre.

ICCF have been supporting a diverse range of football enthusiasts across Leicestershire, who are looking to step into coaching. ICCF have provided a safe learning environment, putting theories into practice and having 1:1 mentorship opportunities. We hope to continue this into 2024 onwards on a wider scale.


Where are we heading

There are 3 primary aims for ICCF:

1. To create football opportunities for young people living Leicester.

2. ICCF will use their training academy to create and build teams which allow the young people to play football at a competitive level

3. Provide a thriving space for young people to explore opportunities in football coaching and refereeing spaces


ICCF Code of Conduct

As a club we take code of conduct seriously.

The aims of the Club shall be to:

  • Offer an opportunity to players to participate in football in a safe environment and the club will always put well-being, safety and enjoyment of each player above everything.
  • Always show respect to everyone involved in the game and encourage fair play.
  • Improve the fitness, skill and technique of young people in football to a high standard.
  • To encourage young people of communities to breakdown stereotypes and barriers.
